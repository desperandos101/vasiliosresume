Piracy Is Bad Argument

Films and DVDs cost money to make. When a company sells a film or DVD, they are doing so in hopes that they can turn a profit. So, when a consumer can access it for free, that means that there will be less of a profit for the company. But what’s the deal, right? Usually you’ll be stealing from giant faceless corporations anyway.
However, behind that brand name, there are people that the company needs to pay. And if the company doesn’t have enough money to pay all their workers, they will either cut the pay or lay off workers altogether. So when you pirate, you hurt more than the company.

Piracy Is Fine Argument

So, say theres this movie that has released. Its marketing campaign was quite iffy. Many people simply don’t wish to commit to the option of purchasing the movie if it didn’t look that good on commercials.
Now say that there is this person who also did not want to commit the price. Instead, they have pirated the movie and watched it. If they heavily enjoyed it, they can recommend it to their skeptical friends. Overall, there’s a net increase in sales of the movie from this word-of-mouth interaction.
